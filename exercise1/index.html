Testing

Released in 2018, A$AP Rocky’s *Testing* is arguably his most ambitious and polarizing project. True to its title, the album served as a platform for sonic experiments, moving away from conventional Trap while retaining heavy bass and psychedelic soundscapes.

The record is built on chaos and collision, incorporating rock, Moby samples, and Grime influences, which resulted in a sprawling, yet often fragmented, vision of modern hip-hop. While inconsistent, its high points, such as "Praise The Lord," solidified Rocky's position as an avant-garde figure.

Long.Live.A$AP

Released in 2013, *Long.Live.A$AP* served as Rocky’s major-label debut, successfully translating his psychedelic Trap sound to a mainstream audience. The album maintained his Houston-influenced dark beats while showing versatility through collaborations with artists like Skrillex and Santigold.

Tracks like "Goldie" and "F**kin' Problems" cemented his status as a fashion-forward, yet musically grounded, star. This record was critical in establishing his reputation for blending Southern production with his New York swagger and experimental tendencies.

Don't be dumb

"Don't Be Dumb" represents more than just a song; it is the working title for A$AP Rocky’s highly anticipated, unreleased studio album. The title encapsulates his sharp, often confrontational, attitude toward the music industry and his experimental approach to his latest body of work.

Beyond the music, Rocky’s partnership as Creative Director for **PUMA's F1 division** solidifies his status as a key figure bridging high fashion, Hiphop, and motorsport culture. This role emphasizes his broader artistic vision, treating fashion and design with the same experimental energy as his music.
